Robust Ramble

Herne Bay to Hazlemere

Section 16 Out

Frinsted to Hucking

OS Map: Explorer 148 Maidstone & the Medway Towns

Total Circular Distance: 9 miles/6 hours

Start: Parking at the church and on-road

Comment: A varied walk in isolated countryside with woodland, grassy valleys, constant slopes and regular distant views. The Hook and Hatchet pub at Hucking is excellent. Open everyday with food. Also offering a camp site. There are no facilities at Frinsted.

With Frinsted church behind, walk back to the T-junction. Here turn right on the road between houses. Soon reach a righthand bend. Leave the road through a metal kissing gate and go up a path between fences. Cross a stile and keep forward to the top of the path and a stile on your right into a pasture.

Cross and turn diagonally left across the field to the top left corner. Ignore a stile off right and find a stile by a metal fieldgate in the corner. Cross to a further stile by a metal fieldgate into a huge sloping pasture.

Go slightly diagonally left down the field to the fence at the bottom. Turn left along this fence passing an old chalkpit on your left. Continue to the very end of the field and cross a stile into trees beyond.

Soon emerge into a field and continue up the edge with a hedge on your left.

At the top, bear left around a huge tree. At the far side go through a white barrier onto a rough farm track. Follow this past old farm buildings. Just past the last house on your left, reach a metal kissing gate on the left. Here turn right by a pole and pass holly bushes to reach a stile.

Cross into the corner pf a vast sloping field. Bear diagonally left down into the valley, pass through a gap   between two lines of trees, and continue up towards a hedge at the top and a wooden telegraph pole.

Emerge on to a road by he pole and turn right for 100 metres. Pass farm buildings on he right and look for a meeting of five gates on your left. Go through the narrow metal pedestrian gate.

The next half mile across a huge field is very difficult. Basically follow the direction of the fingerpost at the start. This goes diagonally right for ¼ mile across and then down to the far opposite corner of the field. Be careful not to drift left.

From the bottom corner (there is a waymark on a post here) go on across towards woods.

Reach a metal fieldgate with an unusual chain stile at the side. Go into the field ahead, with further woodland ahead. Bear diagonally right across to the far opposite  righthand corner and drop down into a wide leafy track.

Reach and cross a stile by a metal fieldgate and cross into a grassy valley. Turn diagonally left, up towards power lines. Pass a chalkpit on the right and turn steeply right upto the very top corner and a metal kissing gate just under power lines.

Go through and upto a stile, although no step (there are handles to allow passage through the electric fence).

Once through go forward carefully, look for a stile on the left hidden behind nettles. There is a metal fieldgate nearby. The stile has no steps.

Enter a field and go diagonally right across to woods at the far side. Exit onto a dirt road and turn right. In 20 metres, around a righthand bend, look for a stile in the hedge on your left.

Cross into a meadow and go forward. Reach a stile in the far corner. Turn right on an enclosed bridleway, uphill by a fence on your left. This soon becomes a hard farm road. Follow this all the way up to a T-junction at the top.

Cross a road to a footpath into he woods opposite. Follow the narrow, overgrown path for some way. Eventually emerge into a field. Turn left along the edge.

Where the wood soon ends with an open field ahead, turn left down the edge to a house on the left. Here turn right on a path across the field under wires.  At far side enter a wood and continue to a road. Turn right up to a junction. Turn left past a house on the left and immediately turn left again into a byway.

Follow this for ½ mile, muddy in places and used by motorbikes. Weave right and left to finally reach a sharp left bend. Here leave the byway and go forward to a wooden fieldgate.

Ignore a bridleway off right and go around the fieldgate to a wide grassy track (not a PROW but this is open. access land). Continue through woods for same way to reach a metal kissing gate in a wire fence at the far side

Do not go through, but turn left on a track along with the fence on your right for ¼ mile. Eventually reach a very large tree with a wooden kissing gate on the right. Go through into a large sloping pasture with information about wild boars ahead.

However do not go forward from the kissing gate but turn 45 degrees right and climb up the field to a wooden kissing gate by a wooden fieldgate at the top.

Go on up a grassy track. This soon weaves through woods before exiting onto a road. Go right a few paces then left,  through a metal swing gate and on up a field edge.

Pass by a house on your left then on with a fence on the left to the top of the field. Go forward to drop down into the next field and go straight across to a wooden pole.

Cross the road to a wooden kissing gate and enter a large field. Look across left to see the roof and white top of the Hook & Hatchet pub in Hucking. Go across to it and the end of the section.

Herne Bay to Hazlemere

Section 16 Return

Hucking to Frinsted

OS Map: Explorer 148 Maidstone & the Medway Towns

Total Circular Distance: 9 miles/6 hours

Start: Parking at the Hook & Hatchet pub

Comment: A varied walk in isolated countryside with woodland, grassy valleys, constant slopes and regular distant views. There are no facilities at Frinsted so take supplies.

With the Hook & Hatchet behind, turn right down the road passing the car park on your right. Stay on the road round right then lefthand bends, then steeply uphill.

Ignore an equestrian gate on left opposite Hucking Court Barn and keep forward to reach Hucking church on the right.

Opposite church, turn left down a narrow enclosed path between hedges. In 10 metres, turn right through a wicket gate and continue through trees.

Reach a metal kissing gate into a pasture and go straight across. Go through a metal kissing gate by a wooden fieldgate at the far side and then forward on a grassy path.

Exit through a wooden kissing gate to a road and cross to a metal kissing gate opposite. Go straight on, up by the hedge on your right.

Exit through a wooden wicket gate and keep on down to a metal fieldgate. Go forward down to the foot of the valley below.

At the bottom bear across left to a metal kissing gate into Gorham Wood. Keep along a basically straight path through the wood for ½ mile.

Ignore all side turnings. On reaching a metal fieldgate halfway, bear left, still on the enclosed forest path. Eventually ignore a stile  off right and continue to a road.

Cross to a footpath into a wood. Soon reach a junction and turn right. At a Y-fork bear right, steeply uphill.

At the top enter a field and go forward, very slightly right of a telegraph pole at the far side. This was very difficult as the path was not reinstated through the crop.

At the far side go under overhead wires to trees around the field edge and a tucked away corner. Go 20 metres right of this corner for a stile into a sloping pasture beyond.

Go straight down the field edge then up to a stile in the top corner. Cross to a road, Bicknor Lane, and turn right for 150 metres.

Pass a metal fieldgate on your left and 30 metres beyond look for a path off left between two stout trees (an unusable rusty metal fieldgate just beyond is the original entrance to the path).

Go up the side of an orchard with a hedge on your right. Keep on this main track for ¼ mile. At the top of a rise stay on the track as it bears left.

In 100 metres, look for a wooden swing gate by a wooden fieldgate on the right. Go through this into a field corner and bear diagonally left across the field (midway between pylons and just right of a house) to the far side.

Exit through the hedge and a wooden swing gate into a large paddock. Continue diagonally across to a stile onto a road just right of buildings.

Turn left up the busy road for a short way. Pass a house and drive on the right. Before the next house on the right climb a bank in the hedge to a metal kissing gate into a field corner. Go diagonally through several gates across three small pastures (some rare breeds here).

Reach a corner with trees and a drive on your right. Cross a flimsy stile out of the last pasture and go forward into a path enclosed between fences(do not join the vehicle drive going off right).

Keep down the path to a stile at the bottom. Cross into a sloping field and bear diagonally right down and across the field to the bottom right corner. Leave the field onto a path dropping down through woods.

Enter a vast sloping field. Bear very slightly right to the far top corner. Here exit by a metal fieldgate out to a road.

Cross to the path opposite. Find a broken stile and continue up the edge of a rough field to a double stile in the top corner.

Go very slightly right down the field ahead to a corner where the straight fence along the bottom of the field meets a wood pushing out into the field. Find a stile.

Cross and continue steeply down. Reach a flimsy stile into a vast pasture. Go all the way up by a hedge on your right to a stile in the top corner.

Cross and go through trees to a corner of a meadow. Keep on, up by the hedge on the right.

Reach a metal fieldgate with stile tucked away to the left and continue up the field, aiming left of houses ahead. Reach a stile onto a road.

Turn right past houses to a T-junction. Cross to a stile and enter a narrow sloping pasture.

Turn sharp left initially along the edge of the pasture, then bear diagonally right across to the top edge and keep along to the far end corner. Bear right through a gap into the next pasture.

Go forward about 20 metres to the end of a hedge coming up the hill. Turn left down this with it on your righthand side. In the bottom corner find a stile and cross to a rising meadow.

Go forward on a feint path. At a Y-fork bear right. Gradually climb the slope to a stile into trees at the top.

Continue up through the trees to a further stile into a  meadow. Bear right along the side across to a stile in the hedge in the far corner.

Cross in to a field and bear diagonally left across to a stile at the far side. Cross and turn left up a wide enclosed path. Cross a stile halfway then reach a metal kissing gate out to a road.

Turn right along the road soon emerging into the centre of Frinsted at a road junction. The church is just ahead at the start of the section.
